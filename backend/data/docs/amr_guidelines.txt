ANTIMICROBIAL RESISTANCE - WHO CLINICAL GUIDELINES

FLUOROQUINOLONES (Ciprofloxacin, Levofloxacin):
- First line for gram-negative urinary and respiratory infections
- Resistance mediated by gyrA/parC mutations and CpxR efflux pumps
- Avoid in renal impairment above certain doses
- Not recommended for MRSA

BETA-LACTAMS (Amoxicillin, Piperacillin-Tazobactam, Augmentin):
- Contraindicated in penicillin allergy
- Resistance via beta-lactamase and ESBL production
- TZP preferred for polymicrobial and hospital-acquired infections
- Augmentin covers beta-lactamase producing organisms

AMINOGLYCOSIDES (Gentamicin, Amikacin):
- Nephrotoxic — strictly avoid in renal impairment
- Resistance via aminoglycoside-modifying enzymes AAC(6')
- Requires therapeutic drug monitoring
- Used as combination therapy for severe sepsis

GLYCOPEPTIDES (Vancomycin):
- Reserved for MRSA and resistant gram-positive organisms
- Monitor renal function closely during therapy
- VRE (Vancomycin-Resistant Enterococcus) emerging globally
- Requires ID specialist approval in most formularies

CARBAPENEMS (Meropenem, Imipenem):
- Last resort for MDR gram-negative infections
- Resistance via carbapenemase production (KPC, NDM-1, OXA-48)
- Requires infectious disease specialist approval
- Associated with C. difficile risk

OXAZOLIDINONES (Linezolid):
- Active against MRSA, VRE, and resistant gram-positives
- No renal dose adjustment required
- Avoid prolonged use due to myelosuppression risk
- Oral bioavailability equivalent to IV

CEPHALOSPORINS (Ceftriaxone):
- Broad spectrum gram-negative coverage
- Resistance via AmpC beta-lactamases
- First line for community-acquired pneumonia

CARD DATABASE KEY RESISTANCE MECHANISMS:
- CpxR: Efflux pump regulator conferring fluoroquinolone resistance
- gyrA mutation: DNA gyrase alteration causing quinolone resistance
- blaKPC: Klebsiella pneumoniae carbapenemase
- blaNDM: New Delhi metallo-beta-lactamase
- mcr-1: Plasmid-mediated colistin resistance
- vanA/vanB: Glycopeptide resistance genes
- AAC(6')-Ib: Aminoglycoside acetyltransferase

TREATMENT PRINCIPLES:
- De-escalate therapy based on culture and sensitivity results
- Consider local antibiogram data for empiric therapy selection
- Always check renal function before aminoglycoside or glycopeptide use
- Patient allergy history must be verified before beta-lactam prescription
- Infection frequency increases risk of resistance development
- Hospital-acquired infections carry higher MDR organism risk
- Elderly patients (>65) require adjusted dosing and increased monitoring
